A native Mac MOV to GIF converter. Drag a short .mov clip into Bench and get an animated GIF back without uploading to a cloud service. Runs locally on your Mac.

What it does

Bench's MOV to GIF tool takes a QuickTime .mov screen recording and converts it to an animated GIF using macOS's native image and video APIs. GIFs are still the most portable format for embedding short UI demonstrations in GitHub issues, Notion pages, Slack threads, and README files — they require no video player and render inline in virtually every context. Bench handles the frame extraction, palette quantization, and GIF encoding locally, so the process is as fast as your disk and CPU allow rather than your upload speed.

Why use Bench's MOV to GIF converter

Web converters for GIF often impose file size limits, require an account, or upload your video before processing it. For recordings that include internal product UI, pre-release features, or anything you wouldn't want on a third-party server, local conversion is the correct default. Bench performs the entire operation on your Mac — the .mov file goes in, the .gif file comes out, and no bytes leave your machine in between.
